About Me

I am a DPhil (PhD) researcher at the University of Oxford, based in the Nuffield Department of Orthopaedics, Rheumatology and Musculoskeletal Sciences (NDORMS), within the Prieto-Alhambra Group for Pharmaco- and Device Epidemiology.

My research sits at the intersection of real-world evidence, genomics, and machine learning. I work with large-scale health databases, UK Biobank genomic data, and graph neural networks to understand and address Long COVID and post-acute sequelae of SARS-CoV-2.

I hold an MSc in Mathematical Sciences from the University of Oxford (where my thesis on the impact of COVID-19 certification was published in Frontiers in Public Health), and a BSc in Mathematics and Physics from Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. During my undergraduate years, I developed computational models of DNA flexibility and phonon transport in semiconductor superlattices at ICMAB-CSIC.

02

Research Areas

Pharmacoepidemiology

Large-scale real-world evidence studies across European health databases using the OMOP Common Data Model, contributing to the DARWIN EU and EHDEN initiatives.

Genomics & GWAS

Genome-wide association studies of Long COVID and post-acute sequelae using UK Biobank, including clustering of symptom phenotypes and identifying genetic determinants.

Machine Learning & GNNs

Graph neural networks (SEAL model) for drug repurposing, predicting novel drug-disease links from biomedical knowledge graphs for Long COVID therapeutics.

Computational Biophysics

Coarse-grained models of DNA flexibility with multimodal correlations between base pairs, and phonon transport simulations in semiconductor superlattices.

03

Selected Projects

A selection of research projects and open-source contributions.

DPhil Research

Clustering Post-Acute COVID-19 Patients

Latent class analysis of symptom phenotypes in post-COVID condition using UK Biobank survey data, followed by genome-wide association studies of each symptom cluster. Includes Bayesian fine-mapping, FUMA annotation, and trait enrichment analysis.

R GWAS UK Biobank Clustering Bayesian
DPhil Research

Drug Repurposing with Graph Neural Networks

SEAL-based link prediction model operating on biomedical knowledge graphs (Open Targets) to identify candidate therapeutics for Long COVID. Integrates drug-target, target-disease, and drug-disease edges with subgraph extraction and graph attention.

Python PyTorch GNN / SEAL Drug Repurposing Docker
DPhil Research

Genetic Determinants of Long COVID & PACS

Genome-wide association studies identifying genetic risk factors for Long COVID and post-acute COVID sequelae in UK Biobank, with multi-ancestry analysis.

R GWAS UK Biobank
MSc Thesis · Publication

COVID-19 Certification Policy Impact Analysis

Master's thesis: difference-in-differences analysis evaluating the impact of COVID-19 passport mandates on cases and hospitalisations in the UK. Published in Frontiers in Public Health.

R Causal Inference Policy MSc Thesis
Collaborative Research

Long COVID Studyathon — Immune & Inflammatory PACS

Multi-database federated study characterising immune and inflammatory post-acute COVID sequelae across DARWIN EU partner databases using OMOP CDM.

R OMOP CDM DARWIN EU Shiny
BSc Thesis · Computational Physics

Multimodal Correlated DNA Model

BSc thesis: coarse-grained Monte Carlo simulation of DNA flexibility with multimodal correlations between base pairs. Published in Nucleic Acids Research.
